## Abstract

Artificial intelligence (AI) has emerged as a transformative tool in HIV research, offering innovative solutions for predicting viral mutations, optimizing drug discovery and formulation design. This review examines current applications and future prospects of AI in HIV management.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Machine Learning**: Classification and prediction models
- **Deep Learning**: Neural networks for complex pattern recognition
- **Drug Discovery**: AI-accelerated compound identification
- **Mutation Prediction**: Forecasting resistance evolution

## Key Research Examples

### Three-Stage AI Framework
1. **Stage 1**: LSTM-Autoencoder generates novel molecular structures
2. **Stage 2**: GDL model evaluates HIV target interactions (integrase, protease, RT)
3. **Stage 3**: Molecular docking simulates binding affinities

### UK Surveillance Data Analysis
- Machine learning on ~55,000 RT sequences
- Discovered six new resistance-associated mutations
- Located near enzyme's active or regulatory pockets

### Drug-Drug Interaction Prediction
- Deep-ARV tool predicts four categories of DDIs
- Uses undersampling and ensemble learning
- Supports clinical drug development
