### Why P-adic is Anti-Correlated with Stability

The genetic code appears to have evolved a **robust-yet-evolvable** structure:

1. **P-adically close codons → Dissimilar amino acids**
   - Single nucleotide changes often produce chemically different amino acids
   - This MAXIMIZES functional diversity from minimal mutations

2. **Error tolerance through diversity**
   - If similar codons encoded similar amino acids, a single error could be catastrophic
   - By anti-optimizing, evolution created a buffer zone

3. **Reproduction over persistence**
   - This strategy favors copy-with-variation (sexual reproduction)
   - NOT persist-and-optimize (individual longevity)

This means: The DIRECT structure of DNA (4 bases) is optimized to make p-adically close changes produce MAXIMALLY different thermodynamic effects.
